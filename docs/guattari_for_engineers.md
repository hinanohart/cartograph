# Guattari for engineers

A two-page reference for working engineers who have not read *Schizoanalytic
Cartographies* (1989). Maps each functor to its operational definition in
Cartograph.

| Functor | Guattari (one line) | Operational definition (Phase 1a/1b) |
|---|---|---|
| **F** (Flows) | continuous a-signifying intensities that pass through assemblages | persistent homology of the velocity field; Phase 1b uses landmark/witness complexes (W1) |
| **Φ** (Machinic Phyla) | the abstract-machinic plane conditioning which assemblages can form | directed graph of co-activating SAE features above a quantile threshold |
| **T** (Existential Territories) | closed semiotic worlds an assemblage inhabits | attention rollout (Abnar & Zuidema 2020) reframed as territorial closure; SSM comparison via Mamba |
| **U** (Universes of Reference) | non-discursive value-domains an assemblage implicitly serves | Pareto frontier over user-supplied scalar objectives |

## Why "diagnose" instead of "explain"

Mechanistic interpretability already explains circuit-level mechanism.
Cartograph asks an orthogonal question: *what kind of subjectivity does this
model produce?* The four functors operationalise that question without
collapsing into circuits.

## What we are not claiming

- We are not claiming the four functors are *the* unique formalisation of
  Guattari's concepts.
- We are not claiming completeness: every release carries a peer review log
  (see `docs/peer_reviews/`) that records remaining construct-validity gaps.
- We are not claiming this replaces close reading of *Cartographies*.
